## Cross-Industry Adaptations

### Section 1 (Executive Summary)
- **Hospitality**: Focus on booking metrics, seasonal trends, OTA dependency
- **E-commerce**: Focus on conversion rate, cart abandonment, product discovery
- **SaaS**: Focus on trial signups, feature adoption, churn prevention
- **Local**: Focus on call volume, quote requests, service area coverage
- **Professional**: Focus on consultation requests, case wins, expertise recognition

### Section 4 (SWOT)
- **Threats**: Always mention industry-specific aggregators
  - Hospitality: Booking.com, Expedia, Airbnb
  - E-commerce: Amazon, eBay, marketplace competitors
  - SaaS: Market leaders, free alternatives
  - Local: Yelp, Angi, HomeAdvisor
  - Professional: Legal directories, referral networks

### Section 9 (Technical SEO)
- **Schema**: Industry-appropriate types
  - Hospitality: Hotel, Resort, BedAndBreakfast, Restaurant
  - E-commerce: Product, Offer, AggregateRating, Review
  - SaaS: SoftwareApplication, Organization, FAQPage
  - Local: LocalBusiness, Service, OpeningHours
  - Professional: ProfessionalService, Attorney, Physician

### Section 10 (Action Plan)
- **Phase 1**: Always focuses on foundational structural pages
- **Phase 2**: Industry-specific growth (seasonal for hospitality, category expansion for e-commerce)
- **Phase 3**: Scale and optimize (internationalization, automation, advanced features)

## Language Adaptations

When generating reports in different languages, adapt:

### Seasonal Events
- **English**: Easter, Bank Holiday, Summer, Black Friday, Cyber Monday
- **Spanish**: Semana Santa, Puente, Verano, Black Friday, Rebajas
- **Italian**: Pasqua, Ponte, Estate, Black Friday, Saldi
- **German**: Ostern, Feiertag, Sommer, Black Friday, Schlussverkauf
- **French**: Pâques, Pont, Été, Black Friday, Soldes

### Section Titles (maintain structure, translate labels)
All templates should use the same 10-section structure, just translate the headings and content.

### Professional Tone
- English: Direct, data-driven, action-oriented
- Spanish: Professional but warm, uses "usted" form
- Italian: Professional with elegance
- German: Precise, formal, structured
- French: Professional with sophistication
